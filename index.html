About Climate Change Group

We're here to bring climate change into your daily conversation.

Copyright © CCG 2017

Are you worried about the changes occurring to our climate, but avoid bringing up the topic? Are you afraid you might offend someone or get into an argument? Concerned you don’t know enough about the science behind climate change to talk about it? Baffled by the fact that the science is clear, yet people continue to insist that either the climate is not changing or, if it is, that the changes are due to natural, as opposed to human, causes?

Luckily, there is a lot of great research out there on how to raise the topic so that people will listen. And it does not require a vast knowledge of climatology.

In our program, we take these climate change communication theories and turn them into practice, teaching you how to raise the issue of climate change comfortably and successfully.

Our programs meet in person in the DC metro area. We request a $20 donation per person to help defray the cost of expenses. For our schedule, or if you have more questions, please email us at info@climatechangegroup.org.

Now that you are comfortable talking about climate change, do you want to do more? But are you unsure what to do? Spending a bunch of time thinking about and debating the right approach? Concerned that there isn’t much one person can do?

We believe that climate change is such a broad issue that there are literally innumerable ways for people to get involved. And everything that you do, can help. So rather than worrying about what the “best” thing is to do, in our Act program we help people step back and look at their skills, interests, resources, time, etc. and help each person figure out what the best thing is for him or her to do.

We want you to leave this program knowing what you can do

  • On a personal level (e.g., solar panels for your home)
  • On a larger level (e.g. community volunteer work of some sort)
  • On a group level (e.g., if your religious organization, club etc. is interested in doing a climate change project together.)

Our programs meet in person in the DC metro area. We request a $20 donation per person to help defray the cost of expenses. For our Act program, we focus on working with groups of people who already know one another, such as college friends, sports teams, clubs, religious groups, etc. We do this so that you can advise and make suggestions to one another. For scheduling, or if you have more questions, please email us at info@climatechangegroup.org.

Meet Anne Schroeder

Until recently, Anne, like so many people, was unaware of the grave threat posed by climate change. However, she has always felt a deep commitment to the community, and has volunteered with The Children’s Inn at NIH and The Fenwick Foundation, an organization that enriches the lives of veterans and the elderly. She is a Board member for The Backpack Project, a non-profit organization dedicated to helping children in under-served areas of Washington, DC.

Her decision to expand her community efforts to include climate change was triggered by a class she recently took on Environmental Science at American University, which opened her eyes to the shocking effects of climate change. This knowledge spurred her to action, and she decided to become involved with fostering the discussion on climate change at Climate Change Group.

Meet Fran Barnes

Fran’s love of the environment began during her childhood in Maine as the youngest of a large family that spent many hours outdoor. Her love of the natural world continues to this day in the work she does as the Assistant Director of The Elements DC. Elements is a winter after-school enrichment program whose primary goal is to connect children to nature.

Fran also has a strong belief in giving back to her community. Until June 2015 she served as Vice President of Programming on the Board of the Parents Council of Washington. Currently she is a tutor for Reading Partners in the DC Public School System, and a Board Member and mentor with City Kids Wilderness Project, which gives at risk kids experiential learning opportunities in the wilderness.

Both her career and volunteer work emphasize fostering communication and developing programs that allow people to grow. She realized she could take the skills used with these groups – non-confrontational communication, patience, strong listening abilities - and apply them to help people talk about climate change.

Meet Tory Ruttenberg

Tory’s fascination with environmental issues began in high school, where her favorite class was environmental chemistry. She majored in American Studies with a focus on Environmental Studies at Yale and then went on to practice environmental law for several years. Most importantly, many of her best memories are of times she has spent in wild natural places.

Since 1997, Tory has worked as a management consultant, focusing on coaching, facilitation, organizational development, and conflict resolution (www.ruttenbergconsulting.com). While she cared about the environment, she wasn’t particularly motivated to do anything. That changed when she read Bill McKibbon’s book, Eaarth, a couple of years ago.

Eaarth fascinated, horrified, and scared her. It also rang true and made her realize that it was time for her to act. But she had no idea what to do. Finally, she realized she could combine her interest in working on climate change with her experience as a coach and facilitator, so she and Fran started ClimateChangeGroup.org.
